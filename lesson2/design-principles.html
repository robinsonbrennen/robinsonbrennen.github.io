Design Principles

Brennen Robinson

Hicks Law

Family Search

familysearch.org

An example of Hicks law on this website is the fact that as soon as the page loads in mobile view or in normal view, immidiately there is a "Create a FREE account" button that is middle center on the page with a bright blue border fill around it. It shows the user exactly what they should do by making it very noticable.

White Space and Clean Design

Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints

churchofjesuschrist.org

The great use of whitespace begins with the logo at the top of the screen when the website is loaded. The logo is completely surrounded from wall to wall by whitespace. Furthermore, as you continue down the screen, each picture has space between it and the one above or below it. The clean design shown on the website through great minimalistic design features like a menu button that keeps options hidden from the page until wanted and a search tool to even out the nearly clean menu bar. Overall, a great example of white space used and clean design.

Visual Heirarchy

Divvy

getdivvy.com

The reason this website shows great visual heirarchy is because as you load up the page your eyes are immidiately drawn from the website name at the top left of the screen, then to the first paragraph explaining what divvy is in a concise way, then the "GET A Demo" button, and then just barely at the bottom it shows the first bolded word of the next thing they want you to scroll to. "Goodbye," catches the attention so that viewers will read on.
